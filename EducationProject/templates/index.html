Average Teacher Salaries For Selected CO Districts

Data indicates that districts of Littleton and Cherry Creek have the highest average salaries. The turnover graph indicated that Littleton and Cherry Creek also had the lowest turnover rate of the districts surveyed. This suggests that retention of employees is highly influenced by salary.

Districts with lower salaries, such as Centennial, Englewood and Colorado Springs have variable levels of turnover, suggesting that other factors, such as working conditions may also influence turnover.

​
Average Test Scores For Selected CO Districts

This chart shows the overall mean score on the Colorado Measures of Academic Success (CMAS) Data indicates there is not much variablility in scores across districts. This is slightly surprising since there are distinct trends for turnover, salaries across districts and budget for each district.

Centennial's data does not show because there are several missing values. In other words, many of their scores for each grade were not reported. In fact, the only scores reported were for English Language Arts and Math for the 6th grade. Their reported scores average out to 714, making Centennial one of the lower performing districts surveyed.

Out of the districts surveyed, the top five highest performing districts are Littleton, Academy 20, Bouldver Valley, Cherry Creek and Adams 12. While the lowest performing districts are Westminster, Englewood, Adams-Arapahoe, Adams 14 and Colorado Springs.

Average Teacher Turnover For Selected CO Districts

Data indicates that teacher turnover varies from district to district. The districts with higher turnover rates, reflecting a higher number of employees that leave after 1 year, generally have lower salaries for teachers and lower overall scores on the CMAS.

The districts with higher turnover rates are Centennial, Adams County 14, Adams-Arapahoe, Denver County and Englewood. Districts with lower retention rates indicate districts that have a higher number of employees that choose to stay within the disrtict. The districts with the lowest turnover rates are Littleton, Cherry Creek, Boulder Valley, Academy 20 and Jefferson County.

Average Budget per Pupil For Selected CO Districts

This chart shows the average spending per pupil for each school district. Most districts surveyed have about $8,000 per pupil spending. However some districts, like Centennial, have budgets as high as $13,000 but has one of the lowest teacher salaries, one of the highest average spending per pupil as well as the highest turnover rate. This suggests that higher budgets do not necessarily translate to higher salaries for teachers nor does it translate to higher test scores.

Additionally, Academy 20 and Littleton have one of the lowest reported spending per pupil. This may mean that money spent towards salaries is a higher factor that influences a districts' overall performance than spending per pupil for resources, curriculums or additional programs. If true, this would make retention of quality teachers even more important than overall spending. However, there are districts like Adams-Arapahoe, with comprable salaries for teachers still continue to perform in the lower range of districts surveyed and continue to experience high turnover when compared to the other districts. This may indicate that other factors such as high turnover and working conditions may still exert a strong influence on a districts performance.
